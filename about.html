Branding Research

Branding can be broken down into Brand Strategy, Brand Identity and Brand Marketing.

Branding strategy allows you to map out all aspects of your business's brand to be able to build a brand identity and then market that to consumers. Aspects such as the purpose of the business, what is unique, how will customers relate and engage with the brand, what tone of voice do you want to communicate, where do you want your business to sit in the market and what are the brand's cultures and values. It is crucial to consider all of these aspects when building a brand.

Consumers play an active role in the success of a brand, they are the group perceiving and engaging with it. It is important to consider the consumers when building a brand, as essentially, it is for them. They hold a lot of power, word-of-mouth and reviews about a brand may either positively or negatively affect its success.

Brand positioning relates to how you set your brand apart from others and position it in the minds of consumers. Ideally, you want your brand to stand out as being reliable and valuable, more so than its competitors. Many strategies can be used to achieve this successfully, such as customer service, convenience-based, price-based, quality-based, differentiation and social media positioning strategies.
